Lindsey, Rebecca. “Climate Change: Global Sea Level.”
NOAA Climate.Gov, 22 Aug. 2023, www.climate.gov/news-features/understanding-climate/climate-change-global-sea-level#:~:text=Global%20mean%20sea%20level%20has,%E2%80%9324%20centimeters.
Overall across the past few years the sea level has been rising over the past few years. Since 1880 the average sea level has risen around 21 to 24 centimeters. On average the sea level has risen by 3.6 millimeters each year from 2006 to 2015, this is 2.5 times as much as it was in the 20th century. This matters to us especially as 30% of the United States population lives on the coast line. This has been caused by added water, primarily from melting glaciers and from thermal expansion.
